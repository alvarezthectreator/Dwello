FAQ

Common Questions

How long does it take to sell my property in Europe?
The average time from listing to completion is 8–14 weeks, though properties in sought-after districts or with competitive pricing can sell in as little as 2–4 weeks. Your agent will set realistic expectations based on current market demand in your area.
What is Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD) and do I need to pay it?
SSD applies to residential properties sold within 3 years of purchase: 12% in Year 1, 8% in Year 2, and 4% in Year 3. If you've held your property for more than 3 years, no SSD is payable. Our calculator in the Seller's Guide above can help you estimate your liability.
Can I sell my HDB flat on the open market?
Yes, provided you have fulfilled the Minimum Occupation Period (MOP) — typically 5 years from the date you received your keys. After the MOP, you can sell on the open resale market. Our agents are well-versed in HDB regulations and will guide you through the process.
How is your commission structured?
Commission is charged as a percentage of the final transacted price — 1% (Essential), 1.5% (Premium), or 2% (Prestige). It is only payable upon successful completion of the sale. There are no upfront fees or retainers, and the valuation is always free regardless of whether you list with us.
Do I need to be present in Europe to sell my property?
Not necessarily. Many of our clients sell remotely via a Power of Attorney (POA). Our team can coordinate all viewings, negotiations, and documentation on your behalf. We recommend working with a local solicitor for the conveyancing, and we can recommend trusted law firms.
What happens if I'm still on a home loan?
Your outstanding mortgage will be discharged from the sale proceeds at completion. Your solicitor coordinates directly with your bank. If there are CPF monies to be refunded, those are also handled at the same time. Your agent will walk you through the net proceeds calculation before you commit to any offer.
